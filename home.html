Aspects™ is a system for creating and viewing natural video from various points of view on events, allowing the user to interactively control the viewpoint and generate new views of dynamic scenes from any 3D position.

Aspects™ allows the viewers to experience never before seen perspectives of live events, breaks the mold of limitations as if they were wearing cameras themselves.

With the ability to navigate freely, Aspects™ gives the user the control to see what you want, changing the way you interact with the video, where the focus of attention can be controlled by the viewers rather than a director, meaning that each viewer may be observing and sharing a unique viewpoint scenario.

The output Multiview Video (MVV) must then be packaged suitably so that the data may be compressed and also so that the users' viewing device may easily access the relevant views to interpolate new views. It is not enough to simply place cameras around the scene to be captured.

In order to acquire the views necessary to allow a high quality rendering of the scene from any angle, several cameras are placed around the scene

Multiview video capture varies from partial (usually about 30 degrees) to complete (360 degrees) coverage of the scene. Therefore, it is possible to output stereoscopic views suitable for viewing with a 3D display or other 3D methods. Systems with more physical cameras can capture images with more coverage of the viewable scene, however, it is likely that certain regions will always be occluded from any viewpoint. A larger number of cameras should make it possible to obtain high quality output because less interpolation is needed.
